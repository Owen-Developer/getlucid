Techniques
Wake Induced
(WILD)
Consciously transition from wakefulness into dreaming, maintaining awareness while the body falls into a dream.
Memory Induced
(MILD)
Use intention and affirmation before sleep to remember dreaming, increasing likelihood of recognizing the dream.
Anchor Technique
Focus on a consistent sensory cue like breathing or a sound to maintain awareness as you enter a dream.
Senses Induced
(SSILD)
Cycle attention through sight, sound, and touch, relaxing awareness and encouraging seamless dream entry easily.
Daily Habits
Dream Journaling
Recording your dreams after waking up, increases awareness, and shows important patterns in your dreams.
Wake Back to Bed
Waking then returning to sleep increases awareness while dreaming, improving lucid dream chances.
Reality Checks
Regularly questioning reality trains awareness, making it easier to recognize dreams and achieve lucidity.
